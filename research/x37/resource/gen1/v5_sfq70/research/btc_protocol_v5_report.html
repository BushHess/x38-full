Frozen system specification

  • Market: BTC/USDT spot, long-only.
  • Signal timing: compute at D1 close, execute at next D1 open.
  • Cost model: 10 bps per side, 20 bps round-trip.
  • Feature: d1_eff_40 = Kaufman-style efficiency ratio over 40 daily bars.
  • Rule: at the start of each calendar year, compute the 70th percentile of d1_eff_40 using all prior history only; stay long when current d1_eff_40 >= threshold, otherwise flat.
  • No leverage, no pyramiding, no regime-specific parameter sets, no extra filter.

Component ablation

The frozen candidate is already the ablated core: one feature, one threshold, one state machine. There is no removable extra layer to defend. The relevant ablation test is therefore family perturbation rather than layer deletion, and the plateau sections above show the result: the family survives nearby lookback and threshold perturbations instead of collapsing outside one cell.

Conclusion

Winner: SF_EFF40_Q70_STATIC

Interpretation: the data preferred a simple “directional risk is worth carrying only when daily trend efficiency is high” rule over more elaborate fast-timing systems.

Why it won: positive post-cost discovery walk-forward, positive 2023–2024 selection holdout, positive reserve/internal slice, broad family plateau, resilience at 50 bps, and lower complexity than the layered alternatives.

Why the label is not CLEAN OOS: the 2025+ reserve range cannot be certified globally untouched across every earlier session, so under Protocol V5 it must be labeled reserve/internal only even though it stayed positive after cost.
